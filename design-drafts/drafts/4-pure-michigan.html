Why I'm running

I'm a limited-license social worker and an urban planner. I've worked in affordable housing and homelessness services here in Detroit. Before that, I spent three years teaching English in Nablus.

That's a long way to come back to a state-house race — and the reason I'm running is straightforward. The people I've worked with, taught, and lived alongside have one thing in common: governments that should have shown up for them, didn't. I'm running for State Representative because District 9 deserves a legislator who has done the work, knows the levers, and won't soften the specifics.

Three priorities for District 9

Specific levers, not slogans. Each of these connects to a real policy fight in Lansing — and a real difference for District 9.

01 / Transit

Invest in Transit to Drive Economic Growth

Fully fund DDOT, advance Bus Rapid Transit on Woodward and Gratiot, and stop charging Detroit drivers more for moving through the same city.

02 / Housing

Realize Housing as a Human Right

Authorize Detroit's Land Value Tax, remove zoning barriers to affordable supply, and fund homelessness solutions that actually keep people housed.

03 / Basic Needs

Fund Programs to Meet and Exceed Basic Needs

Expand benefit enrollment, increase mental health funding, and advocate for Medicaid expansion — because basic dignity should not be a wedge issue.

Auto Insurance Redlining

A September 2025 advocacy report documented an enormous gap in auto insurance costs across Detroit, with drivers in predominantly Black neighborhoods paying many times what their neighbors in Midtown pay — for the same coverage, the same cars, the same roads.

That's not market pricing. It's redlining with a different spreadsheet. Outlawing it statewide is achievable, popular, and overdue.
