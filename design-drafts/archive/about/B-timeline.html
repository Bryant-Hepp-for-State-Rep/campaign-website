About

How I got here

A path from a Detroit upbringing through Nablus and back to the housing programs and city planning offices of HD-9.

Detroit

Born and raised here

Detroit kid, Detroit family. The schools, the buses, the bills. The city's working-class spine is the backdrop for everything that follows.

MSU

B.A. International Relations

Michigan State. Wanted to understand how power moves at scale — how decisions made in distant rooms ended up at our kitchen table.

Nablus

Three years teaching English in the West Bank

In Nablus, I taught English to high school students whose lives were shaped daily by a state that didn't recognize their dignity. It changed how I think about civil rights, governance, and what showing up actually means.

UMich

Dual degrees: Urban Planning & Social Work

Graduate school at the University of Michigan. The two programs in parallel were deliberate — I wanted both the systems lens (planning) and the person-by-person lens (social work).

Detroit

Affordable housing & homelessness services

Returned home and went to work in the systems I'd studied. Coordinated entry. Housing-first. The actual mechanics of getting a person into a home that stays a home.

2026

Running for State Representative, District 9

I'm running because the people I've worked with, taught, and lived alongside have one thing in common: governments that should have shown up for them, didn't. District 9 deserves a legislator who has done the work and won't soften the specifics.
