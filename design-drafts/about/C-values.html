About

What I believe, and why

Four convictions, each backed by what I've actually done. These aren't slogans — they're the lens I'll bring to every vote in Lansing.

Housing is a human right.

I work in affordable housing and homelessness services in Detroit. I've sat across from people whose only barrier to a stable life was a deposit they couldn't make, a unit that didn't exist, or a system that didn't believe them.

Housing-first works. Coordinated entry works. The Detroit Land Value Tax — sitting in Lansing waiting for state authorization — would shift the cost of vacancy onto the speculators who profit from it. These aren't theories. They're tools we have, and aren't using.

Transit moves communities.

I'm an urban planner. The auto-insurance redlining that opens this campaign is what happens when a city forces everyone into a car and then prices the car like a luxury for half the population.

Fully funding DDOT, advancing Bus Rapid Transit on Woodward and Gratiot, restoring state revenue sharing — every one of these moves is technically straightforward. The obstacle is political will. I'll bring it.

Government should show up.

I'm a limited-license social worker. The clinical work taught me what people actually need from public services when they're at the worst point of their year, and how often the answer is just somebody who picks up the phone.

That sounds small. It's not. It's the difference between a benefit enrollment that takes two weeks and one that takes nine months. Mental health funding, expanded benefit access, Medicaid expansion advocacy, ending utility shutoffs through a fully-funded Lifeline Plan — basic dignity isn't a wedge issue.

Foreign policy is local.

I spent three years teaching English in Nablus. I watched a state that didn't recognize my students' dignity decide every detail of their day — and I watched U.S. tax dollars underwrite it.

State representatives don't set foreign policy. But the human-rights principles I learned on those streets — that governments owe the people they govern, and that civil rights are indivisible — show up in every state-level fight: ICE access to schools, immigrant access to services, federal intimidation of communities here. These I will set, and lead on.
