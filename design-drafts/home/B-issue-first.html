In District 9 · September 2025 advocacy report

Black neighborhoods in Detroit pay up to 5× more for the same auto insurance.

I'm Bryant Hepp — limited-license social worker, urban planner, and a Democrat running for State Representative to outlaw auto insurance redlining and rebuild the public services District 9 has been denied.

Context

Same coverage. Same cars. Same roads. A driver in Midtown pays a fraction of what a driver pays two miles east, in a predominantly Black neighborhood. The difference isn't risk — it's the ZIP code.

That's not market pricing. It's redlining with a different spreadsheet. Outlawing it statewide is achievable, popular, and overdue.

Who's running

Bryant Hepp

Limited-license social worker. Urban planner. Three years teaching English in Nablus. I've worked in affordable housing and homelessness services here in Detroit.

I'm running because the people I've worked with, taught, and lived alongside have one thing in common: governments that should have shown up for them, didn't.
