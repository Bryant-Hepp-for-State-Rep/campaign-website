A letter from Bryant Hepp · Detroit · 2026

Detroit, I'm running for State Representative because driving costs too much — and that's only the beginning.

A driver in Midtown pays a fraction of what a driver two miles east pays for the same auto insurance. Same coverage. Same car. Same roads. The difference isn't risk — it's the ZIP code. Black neighborhoods in this city pay up to five times more for car insurance than predominantly white neighborhoods. That's not market pricing. That's redlining with a different spreadsheet, and it's the most expensive line item in too many household budgets.

I'm a limited-license social worker and an urban planner. I've worked in affordable housing and homelessness services here in Detroit. Before that, I spent three years teaching English in Nablus. The people I've worked with, taught, and lived alongside have one thing in common: governments that should have shown up for them, didn't.

[ Photo placeholder — Bryant in the district ]

District 9 deserves a legislator who has done the work, knows the levers, and won't soften the specifics. That means outlawing auto insurance redlining statewide. Fully funding DDOT and advancing Bus Rapid Transit on Woodward and Gratiot. Authorizing Detroit's Land Value Tax. Funding mental health and homelessness solutions that actually keep people housed. Protecting immigrants from federal intimidation. Stopping DTE from buying influence with the public's money.

I'm running because nobody is owed a representative who shows up. We have to elect them. I'm asking you to help me become yours — and then to hold me to every word above.

Bryant Hepp — Democrat for Michigan State House, District 9
